Description

A classic West African dish made with long-grain rice simmered in a rich tomato, pepper, and spice blend. Slow-cooked to perfection, it's smoky, flavorful, and perfect with chicken, fish, or plantains.

Ingredients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice:3 cups (rinsed)
  • Vegetable oil:1/4 cup
  • Tomato paste:3 tbsp
  • Fresh tomatoes:4 large (or 1 can plum tomatoes)
  • Red bell peppers:2 large
  • Scotch bonnet (habanero) pepper:1 (adjust to spice level)
  • Onions:2 large (1 for blending, 1 for cooking)
  • Garlic:3 cloves
  • Ginger:1-inch piece (or 1 tsp ground ginger)
  • Chicken stock:3 to 4 cups (or water + stock cube)
  • Bay leaves:2
  • Seasoning cube (Maggi/Knorr):2
  • Thyme:1 tsp dried

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Blend Base: Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, Scotch bonnet, onion, garlic, ginger.

  2. Cook Stew Base: Heat oil, fry onions, add tomato paste 2 to 3 min. Add blended mix, cook 15 to 20 min until oil separates.

  3. Season:Add thyme, curry, seasoning cubes, bay leaves, salt/pepper. Simmer 5 min.

  4. Add Rice and Stock: Rinse rice, stir into stew, add stock to cover by 1 inch. Cover tightly.

  5. Cook: Simmer on low 25‚Äì30 min, stir midway. Add water if needed. Finish with butter (optional).
